What does camm stand for?

Camm is an artificial name generated by the initials of its four founding team members.

Who invented camm? What’s the story behind it?

Camm was founded in 2019 by German entrepreneur Christoph Bertsch, who was searching for a real sustainable material solution for one of his health & lifestyle companies. He came across an available technology based on polyvinyl alcohol, known from the plastic wrapper of dishwasher tabs. The innovation was the combination of PVOH and organic additives, which creates a stable and pliable material. Different formulations allow camm to be used for a wide range of potential applications.
Having found a suitable technology, Christoph built an infrastructure of employees, partners, companies and investors, creating a competitive web of chemical engineering, mechanical engineering, and raw material sourcing. This allows a significant scale-up of the camm material and its commercialization, while ensuring a high level of sustainability.

What is camm?

Camm is a sustainable alternative to plastics and composite packaging. This material is set to be fully bio-based, since it can be made from bio-waste that stems from renewable materials. Camm is certified water-soluble, home compostable and easily recyclable. IF it ends up as litter in the worst case, camm decomposes without leaving behind any substances harmful to the environment – not even nano- or microplastics. This makes camm stand apart from other polymers. And with its versatile properties and various end-of-life scenarios, camm will be capable of replacing plastics and composite materials at scale in all kinds of industries and applications, across many countries and regions. Essential features include:

  • 100% biodegradable on land and in water (including marine environments)
  • 100% water soluble
  • Decomposes without leaving any nano- or microplastics behind
  • Home compostable
  • Recyclable (solely camm material)
  • Protects paper fibers (camm-coated paper) for recycling, thereby supporting a circular economy
  • 100% free of toxins
  • Food contact approved
  • Can be processed with industry standard technologies (e.g. coating, extrusion, injection molding)
  • Offers a wide range of customizable barrier properties (e.g. gas, grease and vapor)
  • Fully bio-based by 2025

Although camm is a polymer we do not consider it a plastic, as it works in synch with natural cycles and leaves no pollution behind.
